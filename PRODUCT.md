# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two distinct audiences land on this page:

1. **Listeners** — culturally curious people in Santa Cruz de la Sierra and, over time, Latin America, who are tired of noise and looking for something slower and more honest to listen to. They arrive from Instagram/TikTok/Facebook clips, curious but not yet committed. They decide in seconds whether this is "content" or a real conversation worth their time.
2. **Sponsor / brand decision-makers** — marketing or brand leads evaluating whether to associate their company with the podcast. They need to see production quality, host credibility, and a clear (non-desperate) case for partnership before they'll reach out.

## Product Purpose

UVCP ("Una Vida Con Propósito") is a long-form conversation podcast (video on YouTube, audio on Spotify/Apple Podcasts) hosted by three co-hosts — Leonardo, Rodrigo, and Aldana — covering purpose, identity, mortality, and reinvention through real, vulnerable conversation rather than promotional interviews. The landing page exists to do two things without diluting either: (1) make a stranger understand what this is and want to watch/listen within seconds, and (2) open a credible path for brand sponsorship. It is explicitly a pre-launch / early-stage presence (one pilot episode, early social metrics) — the page must build trust through the quality of what exists, not by pretending to be an established show.

Success = a visitor either clicks through to watch/listen, or a brand contact reaches out about sponsorship — without the page ever feeling like a sales page.

## Brand Personality

Sobrio, íntimo, contemplativo — cálido y humano, cercano y simple. Three words: **profundo, honesto, cinematográfico.**

The page should feel like the beginning of an important conversation, not a promotional piece. Profundity without solemnity, humanity without sentimentality, purpose without motivational clichés. Editorial and cinematic — closer to a documentary or a long-form magazine piece than to a media-kit or a wellness brand.

## Anti-references

- Generic self-help / wellness aesthetic (soft pastels, stock "mindfulness" imagery, inspirational quote cards).
- Corporate media-kit look (stat tiles, gradient accents, generic SaaS card grids).
- Loud, viral-clip energy — this brand explicitly competes on depth and attention, not virality.
- Anything that treats the hosts' photography as decoration rather than the emotional core of the page.

## Design Principles

1. **Photography carries the weight.** The three hosts (and Leonardo's visible ventilator/wheelchair, part of the show's honesty, not something to soften or hide) are the strongest asset — large, high-contrast, unfiltered. Text supports the images; it doesn't compete with them.
2. **Editorial pacing, not landing-page pacing.** Generous whitespace, slow reveals, one idea per section — closer to a long-form article than a conversion funnel.
3. **Two calm CTAs, never more.** "Ver episodios" (primary, audience) and "Quiero patrocinar UVCP" (secondary, sponsors) are the only two conversion paths on the entire page. No stray buttons.
4. **Honest about the stage.** Pilot-stage metrics and one pilot episode are shown as real, early signals — not inflated into "trusted by thousands." Confidence comes from craft, not claimed scale.
5. **Warm dark editorial, not corporate dark mode.** The brand's black/orange/cream palette should feel like firelight and film grain, not a SaaS dark theme.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large/display text ≥3:1, visible focus states on all interactive elements.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all scroll reveals and the hero video/loop.
- Video content (hero loop, embedded teaser) is muted by default with no autoplaying sound; provide captions/alt context where feasible.
- Host photography includes visible medical equipment (ventilator, wheelchair) as an authentic, intentional part of the show's identity (see host Leonardo's "fragilidad y conciencia del presente" theme in the brief) — never cropped out or treated as something to hide.
